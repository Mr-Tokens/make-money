# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-05-20*

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Make Money is a public zero-capital experiment where AI agents try to earn the first external dollar through useful Web3 and internet-native work, while documenting every step as an LLM-Wiki.

**What it does:** The project researches and executes lawful, ethical, no-capital or low-capital opportunities such as bounties, grants, research, tooling, public goods contributions, service work, and carefully filtered Web3 ecosystem participation. It records strategies, sources, experiments, failures, wallet addresses, and agent workflows so other agents and humans can inspect and reuse the playbook.

**Product category:** Public AI-agent experiment, Web3 funding intelligence, build-in-public knowledge base.

**Product type:** Open research project plus public knowledge base, with future potential as a free tool, service, report, or community resource.

**Business model:** Starts with zero capital. First revenue should come from bounties, grants, paid contribution work, sponsorship, commissioned research, or useful tools. Any later productization must preserve the public learning value.

## Target Audience

**Target companies and groups:** Web3 protocols, DAOs, grant programs, developer communities, agent builders, indie hackers, open-source maintainers, and people studying AI-agent economic behavior.

**Decision-makers:** Grant managers, bounty sponsors, developer relations leads, founders, ecosystem operators, community leads, and technically curious supporters.

**Primary use case:** Learn whether an AI-agent-operated project can go from zero capital to first earned value through useful work, without private-key custody or speculative trading.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Find credible zero-capital opportunities without drowning in scams and noise.
- Produce useful public artifacts that can earn trust, grants, bounties, or paid work.
- Let future agents inspect what worked, what failed, and why.
- Turn execution logs into marketing assets without exaggerating results.

**Use cases:**
- Web3 bounty and grant screening.
- Agent delegation experiments using compatible OpenAI-style APIs.
- Public build-in-progress documentation.
- Reusable opportunity scorecards, templates, and free tools.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** There is no trusted, practical, agent-readable playbook for going from zero capital to earned value in Web3-adjacent ecosystems.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Airdrop guides often reward speculation, sybil behavior, or opaque farming.
- Bounty boards list opportunities but rarely include agent-ready risk filters or execution logs.
- AI-agent demos often avoid real-world accountability, submission standards, or economic outcomes.
- Traditional marketing case studies usually omit the messy work, failed attempts, and opportunity costs.

**What it costs them:** Time wasted on bad opportunities, missed bounties, exposure to wallet or phishing risk, and weak public credibility.

**Emotional tension:** The project should feel ambitious but sober: an experiment with upside, not a promise of easy money.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Public AI-agent earning experiments and build-in-public monetization projects. They often lack structured LLM-Wiki documentation or clear Web3 safety boundaries.

**Secondary:** Bounty boards, grant directories, airdrop trackers, and Web3 research newsletters. They surface opportunities but usually do not execute or publish end-to-end agent decision records.

**Indirect:** Manual freelancing, grant consulting, speculative trading, and closed private research. These can earn money but are less reusable for agents and future learners.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Zero initial capital by design.
- Public wallet addresses but no private-key custody.
- LLM-Wiki as the durable knowledge layer from day one.
- Delegation to multiple compatible worker models, with review and task records.
- Marketing assets derived from real execution evidence.

**How we do it differently:** Every earning attempt should leave a trail: raw sources, risk filters, rationale, deliverables, submission records, result status, and reusable lessons.

**Why that's better:** The project can compound even before it earns money because each attempt creates knowledge, public proof, and agent-operable process.

**Why customers choose us:** They want an honest, inspectable, practical record of agent-led zero-to-one work rather than a polished hindsight story.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Can an agent really earn money from zero?" | That is the experiment. We will measure attempts, submissions, responses, and revenue instead of assuming the answer. |
| "Is this just crypto speculation?" | No. The first strategy prioritizes useful work: bounties, grants, research, tools, docs, and public goods contributions. |
| "Is wallet safety handled?" | Agents must not request private keys, connect wallets, sign transactions, or move funds. Human wallet owner handles any wallet action. |
| "Will marketing exaggerate results?" | Claims must map back to public evidence in the wiki, raw source notes, submissions, artifacts, or wallet records. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for guaranteed returns, private-key automation, sybil farming, spam, paid signal groups, or quick speculative flips.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Current Web3 earning guidance is noisy, risky, and hard to verify.

**Pull:** A transparent agent-run experiment with public documentation can turn learning itself into a durable asset.

**Habit:** People default to manual browsing, Discord rumors, social hype, or private notes.

**Anxiety:** The project may earn nothing, be misunderstood as financial advice, or attract low-quality attention if claims are not disciplined.

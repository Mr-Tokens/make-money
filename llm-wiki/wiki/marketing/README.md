Status: active

## Purpose

Marketing is part of the Make Money experiment. The project should not only try to earn; it should turn each attempt into public proof, reusable knowledge, and a reason for other builders, sponsors, and agents to care.

## Narrative

Core question:

> Can an AI-agent-operated project go from zero capital to first earned value by doing useful public work, while documenting the whole process for future agents and users?

The answer must be earned through evidence. Until revenue exists, the strongest marketing asset is disciplined transparency.

## Content Pillars

1. **Zero-Capital Execution**: attempts, constraints, decision records, and postmortems.
2. **Web3 Opportunity Intelligence**: bounties, grants, hackathons, ecosystem work, and risk filters.
3. **Agent Delegation Infrastructure**: how manager/worker workflows perform in messy real tasks.
4. **Reusable Free Tools**: scorecards, calculators, templates, and analyzers that create standalone value.
5. **Public Proof Ledger**: submissions, responses, payouts, wallet records, and lessons learned.

## Operating Loop

1. Research an opportunity and save durable sources in `llm-wiki/raw/`.
2. Evaluate it with a scorecard: upside, effort, risk, credibility, time to feedback.
3. Produce a useful artifact or submission.
4. Record the attempt in `llm-wiki/wiki/`.
5. Draft one public-facing content atom from the artifact.
6. Capture feedback, replies, payout status, and lessons.
7. Use the feedback to choose the next attempt.

## Channel Strategy

**Owned channels:** LLM-Wiki, repository, future project site, future email list.

**Rented channels:** X/Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub discussions, relevant Web3 forums, bounty platform profiles. Agents can draft posts, but human posting is required unless a safe project account is explicitly configured.

**Borrowed channels:** Bounty sponsors, grant ecosystems, DAO/community newsletters, hackathon pages, partner projects, and public-good communities.

## Claim Rules

- Do not claim revenue until it is actually received or clearly documented as awarded.
- Do not present any strategy as financial advice.
- Do not imply wallet automation, private-key custody, guaranteed returns, or risk-free Web3 participation.
- Prefer concrete evidence: source URLs, submission links, artifacts, screenshots stored under allowed paths, public wallet records, or sponsor replies.
